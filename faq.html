What is a FAQ Page?
An FAQ page is a page that contains the questions and answers to some common questions users may have.
What are the academic requirements?
You need a major GPA of 3.2 and an overall GPA of 3.0. The major GPA includes all courses that are required of the major, both prerequisites and courses for the major.
To give us the ability to assess your work and for you to be comfortable that you want a master's degree, you must have completed at least SEVEN Computer Science courses at the 400-level or higher by the end of your junior year. This can include both required and distribution courses for your major.
When is the application deadline?
Application to the program will be in the spring semester of your junior year. The application materials are to be submitted to Graduate Student Services by the beginning of the fall registration period.
When will an admissions decision be made?
The decision on admission will be made before the end of classes spring semester your junior year.
What are the required application materials?
Name, PID and contact information
Names of two CS faculty members who will serve as your primary references. The admissions committee may choose to contact any other faculty member as well.
A personal statement of purpose, maximum one page, that explains your objectives in pursuing the MS degree.
Resume with work experience, activities, honors, etc.
Once accepted to the program, is it possible to defer admission?
Admission to the master's portion of the program cannot be deferred. Master's work must begin the semester following the completion of the B.S. degree.
How many times can a student apply?
Students are allowed to apply to the program only once.
Is there an application fee?
There is no application processing fee for this program.
Is it possible to submit the application by hard copy?
No, the entire application process must be online.
